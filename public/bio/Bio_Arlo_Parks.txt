Anaïs Oluwatoyin Estelle Marinho known professionally as Arlo Parks, is a British singer-songwriter and poet. Her debut studio album, Collapsed in Sunbeams, was released in 2021 to critical acclaim and peaked at number three on the UK Albums Chart. It earned her nominations for Album of the Year, Best New Artist and Best British Female Solo Artist at the 2021 Brit Awards.
Anaïs was born on 9 August 2000 and raised in Hammersmith, West London. She is half Nigerian, quarter Chadian and quarter French. Marinho learned to speak French before she did English.
Parks chose her stage name in the manner of King Krule and Frank Ocean. In 2018, she began uploading demos to BBC Music Introducing which caught the attention of BBC Radio 1.
She made her solo debut when she released the song "Cola" and announced the release of her debut EP, Super Sad Generation. She told Line of Best Fit that the song is "a reminder that betrayal is inevitable when it comes to pretty people that think flowers fix everything."  The song flourish thanks to her creative writing background, with her delicate tone and soft crackle of vinyl. 
By November 2019, the song had amassed over three million streams on Spotify.
On the 11th May of 2020 she won the Breakthrough Artist Award at the 2021 Brit Awards.
