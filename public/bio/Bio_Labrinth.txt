Timothy Lee McKenzie known professionally by his stage name Labrinth, is a British singer, songwriter, rapper and record producer. He has been referred to as “one of the most important British musicians of his generation”.
He was born and raised in Hackney, London. He is of Jamaican and Canadian descent and comes from a family of musicians. Their home was filled with the sound of American gospel music, which their parents loved. When young, he and his eight siblings formed a band called Mac 9.
After initially pursuing a career in music production, Simon Cowell signed him to his record label Syco Music as a recording artist. Labrinth became the first singer who Cowell signed without having competed on a talent show in six years.
Labrinth has released two solo albums: Electronic and Imagination & the Misfit. In 2018, he formed the supergroup LSD with Australian singer-songwriter Sia and American DJ and producer Diplo. He has also collaborated with a number of artists as a singer, songwriter, and producer including Beyoncé, Kanye West, The Weeknd and many more.
Timothy composed the score for HBO drama series Euphoria and The Lion King - Live action's soundtrack with Beyoncé and Ilya Salmanzadeh. 
He has achieved nominations at Grammy, Golden Globe, Primetime Emmy and Critics' Choice, winning a Primetime Emmy and a NAACP Image Award.
